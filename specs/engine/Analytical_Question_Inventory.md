All diagnostic questions organized by category, separated by source.

## 1. Vacancy & Turnover

### From System Concept

- How long every vacancy lasts, broken into phases: how long from move-out to make-ready completion, how long from completion to first showing, how long from listing to lease signing, how long from lease signing to move-in
- Which phase is the bottleneck for each unit and across the property
- Which units are chronically problematic — keep going vacant, keep taking longer than others
- Whether the property is pre-leasing units before they go vacant
- What the total cost of each vacancy is in lost rent
- How vacancy duration and volume compare across unit types, buildings, and time periods
- Whether vacant units that my field audit found NOT ready are being listed as available
- Whether completed make-ready work orders match the actual condition I observed on-site
- Seasonal patterns in vacancy volume and duration
- Whether units that my field audit found in poor condition are the same ones with the longest vacancy durations and the worst listing quality — and if so, what vendor did the make-ready work and how much was spent on it
- Whether units with long vacancies also had slow leasing team response times in the CRM and poor mystery shop results, pointing to a leasing problem on top of a turnover problem

### From Gemini Research

- What is the specific bottleneck phase in the vacancy lifecycle (move-out to make-ready, or listing to lease)? — Sources: PM, Field Validation
- Are units marked "ready" in the PM system physically showable and clean for prospects? — Sources: PM, Unit Audit
- What is the total lost rent per vacancy based on the actual daily market rent? — Sources: PM, Financials
- What is the delta between physical occupancy and economic occupancy (indicating concession or delinquency issues)? — Sources: PM, Financials
- What is the average number of days a unit remains vacant before being leased? — Sources: PM
- Does the "Trend %" (future occupancy) for 30, 60, and 90 days align with the current marketing spend? — Sources: PM, CRM
- What is the total annual economic vacancy loss (days vacant x daily market rent) across the portfolio? — Sources: PM, Financials
- Is there a concentration of "Shadow Vacancy" where units are held off-market for non-budgeted renovations? — Sources: PM, Unit Audit
- What is the RevPAU (Revenue Per Available Unit) and how does it trend over 12 months? — Sources: PM, Financials
- How many units are currently in a "non-revenue" status (models, employee units, guest suites) and is this justified by performance? — Sources: PM, Unit Audit

### From Gemini Research (Expanded)

- What is the "Aged Vacancy" percentage (units vacant >60 days) and does it correlate with specific floor plans or view premiums? — Sources: PM
- What is the "Pre-Leasing Velocity" for units currently on notice compared to the actual move-out date? — Sources: PM, CRM
- Does the "Trend %" (future occupancy) fluctuate significantly when marketing spend is held constant, suggesting a retention-driven vacancy risk? — Sources: PM, Financials

### From Gemini Forensic Diagnostics

- Does "Days Vacant" correlate with a low photo quality assessment score for that specific floor plan? — Sources: PM Vacancy List × Intake Photo Quality Assessment

- Does the lease expiration concentration match the months where submarket vacancy is at its historical peak, creating a yield management failure? — Sources: PM Lease Expirations × Intake Market Context
- Does pre-lease percentage correlate with website quality score and mobile app capabilities, indicating whether the property is capturing sight-unseen leases? — Sources: Intake Conversion Metrics × Intake Website Quality × Intake Mobile App
- Is vacancy loss disproportionately high for renovated units that lack digital/social media activity to highlight them? — Sources: PM T12 Financials × Intake Unit Amenities × Intake Digital & Social Presence

## 2. Make-Ready & Maintenance

- Whether maintenance capacity is keeping up with demand or creating a backlog
- Whether maintenance issues are concentrated in specific buildings, unit types, or systems
- Whether the vendors with the slowest completion times or highest costs are the same ones whose work my field audit found incomplete or substandard — and whether those units are the ones sitting vacant longest

## 3. Leasing Performance

### From System Concept

- How many leads the property is getting, from which sources, and at what cost per source
- How fast and how well each lead is being responded to — first contact time, response quality
- How many leads convert to tours, how many tours convert to applications, how many applications convert to leases
- How each leasing agent performs individually on every conversion metric
- Which traffic sources are actually producing signed leases, not just inquiries
- How the property's leasing experience compares to competitors — based on my mystery shops of the subject property AND competitor properties
- Whether follow-up is happening, how many touches, over what timeframe, and whether it's effective
- What my mystery shop revealed about the tour experience, staff professionalism, needs assessment, urgency creation, and closing ability
- What recently moved-in residents said about their leasing experience, what attracted them, and what almost made them choose somewhere else
- Whether the agents with the lowest conversion rates in the CRM are the same ones who performed poorly in the mystery shop — and whether the traffic sources feeding those agents are the most expensive ones
- Whether properties where competitors scored better on mystery shops are also the ones winning prospects that the subject property lost in the CRM pipeline

- Is the leasing staff using personalized video or virtual tour tools to close remote leads? — Sources: CRM, Mystery Shop

## 16. Cohort Profiling & Pattern Recognition

For each entity type, the engine ranks all entities by performance, groups them into top / middle / bottom cohorts, and answers the following questions per cohort. The goal is not just to identify who is best and worst, but to understand why, what's missing, and what specific changes would have the greatest impact at every tier.

**Output requirement:** Every cohort analysis must list the actual candidates — the specific units, agents, vendors, deals, etc. by name or ID — not just aggregate observations about the group. The consultant needs to see exactly which entities are in each tier.

### Leasing Agents

**Top cohort:**
- What do the top-performing leasing agents share across all available data — conversion rates, mystery shop scores, follow-up cadence, tenure, training certifications, coaching frequency, assigned traffic sources, and scheduling methods?
- Where can the top-performing agents still improve, and which of those improvements would have the greatest impact on overall leasing performance?

**Middle cohort:**
- What do the middle-performing leasing agents share across all available data?
- What differentiates the middle from the top — what do they lack or do differently in conversion rates, follow-up behavior, mystery shop scores, or traffic source assignment?
- What differentiates the middle from the bottom — what do they have or do that the lowest performers do not?
- Which of those differences would have the greatest impact on performance if addressed?

**Bottom cohort:**
- What do the lowest-performing leasing agents share across all available data — conversion rates, mystery shop scores, follow-up cadence, tenure, training, assigned traffic sources?
- What do the lowest-performing agents lack that both the top and middle performers have?
- Which of those gaps would have the greatest impact on performance if addressed?

**Data completeness:**
- What data is missing or incomplete for each cohort — are mystery shop scores absent, CRM entries inconsistent, training records undocumented, or follow-up activity untracked — and does data completeness itself correlate with performance tier?
